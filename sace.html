The Adelaide Nunga Court is a specialized court within the South Australian Magistrates Court system that provides culturally appropriate and sensitive legal services to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples. The court aims to reduce the over-representation of Indigenous Australians in the criminal justice system by addressing underlying issues such as poverty, social disadvantage, and intergenerational trauma. The court provides a range of services, including diversionary programs, support and referral services, and alternative sentencing options that incorporate Indigenous customary law and cultural practices. The Adelaide Nunga Court also promotes community engagement and consultation to ensure that Indigenous peoples are involved in the decision-making process and have a voice in the justice system.
